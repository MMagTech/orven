The frantic edition

You asked for double. Here it is: seventeen plugins, 47 observations, one genuinely bad night — a failed offsite backup, a container that won't hold, a disk running hot, two plugins silent. If the Broadsheet can make this readable in minutes without ranking a single thing, the identity is proven.

The mechanism

Headline size is a unit of measurement

Same rule as Sprint 02, now with three tiers instead of one. Volume assigns the tier mechanically: the busiest beat leads the page in two columns; five-to-seven-observation beats get band two; three-to-four get band three; everything smaller is one dotted line in “The record in brief.” The reader learns to read headline size the way they read a thermometer — bigger type means more happened, and never anything else. Note what stays constant under load: one page, fixed section order within each band, silence boxed at the bottom, and an explicit end.

Where it finally breaks

An honest note on the ceiling

This composition holds to roughly 60–70 observations. Past that, nothing collapses — the bands simply repeat and the page grows taller, which is exactly how a newspaper has always handled a big news day: more pages, same grammar. The failure mode to guard against isn't length; it's a morning where every beat is busy and the tier system flattens (six beats all with 7+ observations). Even then the rule stays honest — several equal headlines side by side is a truthful picture of a night when everything happened at once.
