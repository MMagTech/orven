Orven Product Design Studio · Sprint 02

The heavy morning

Your question — how do the survivors hold up when twelve plugins all have something to say? Below: the Broadsheet and the Observatory under real load (a morning of 21 observations), the Broadsheet on a quiet day, and one reconciliation that may let you keep both. One rule governs everything here.

The governing rule

Prominence follows volume, never importance

A newspaper scales through mixed density: the story with the most to tell gets the headline and column inches; single facts go to a digest column. Normally an editor decides that by judging importance — which Orven must never do. But the same visual grammar works on a mechanical rule instead:

The plugin with the most observations gets the most space. A one-fact morning is one line in the briefs column. Nothing is ever promoted because it "matters" — only because more of it happened.

This is arithmetic, not opinion — the reader can verify it every morning, which is exactly what builds trust. It also means the layout breathes with the day: a heavy morning composes itself into a full page; a quiet one contracts without embarrassment. Sections always appear in the same fixed order, so position on the page never implies a verdict either.

Study A

The Broadsheet, fully loaded

Twelve plugins, 21 observations. Three devices absorb the load: an index line under the dateline (the whole morning in one glance), a lead well earned by volume (containers had the busiest night), and an “In brief” rail where every one-fact plugin gets exactly one dotted line. Silence keeps its own boxed corner — the one thing a newspaper never does is forget a beat reporter who didn't file.

Study C

The Observatory, fully loaded

The rail scales in the opposite way: it doesn't paginate load, it draws it. Twenty-one observations make the night's shape more legible, not less — the 4 AM cluster is visibly a cluster. Two devices carry the weight: burst events collapse into grouped marks (three dots, one entry), and undated facts move off the rail entirely into a standing observations block, fixing Sprint 01's flagged weakness.

Study D

One product, two lights

You chose one warm paper concept and one cool night concept — which might not be a conflict at all. The Broadsheet supplies the structure: masthead, dateline, volume-led composition. The Observatory supplies the night palette: its deep blue-black ground, cool grays, and pale-blue accents become the Broadsheet's dark mode — same page, different light. (This also happens to be the one kind of dark mode Orven's constitution permits: colors only.) The rail itself could remain a second view for reading the night chronologically — but the identity would be one paper.
